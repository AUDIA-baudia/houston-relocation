Know the Franchise

HOUSTON TEXANS:
THE FULL STORY

Everything Melissa needs to know before sitting down with President Mike Tomon. From the heartbreak of losing the Oilers to the rise of a new franchise — and the people who built it.

The Houston Oilers — Where It Started

Founded 1960 by Bud Adams as a charter AFL franchise
Glory: Won the first two AFL Championships (1960, 1961)
Earl Campbell Era (1978): Heisman winner, Rookie of the Year, "Luv Ya Blue" phenomenon
Warren Moon Era (1984-1993): Run and Shoot offense, 7 consecutive Pro Bowls
The Heartbreak: Bud Adams relocated the team to Tennessee after 1996 season over stadium disputes
Void: Became Tennessee Oilers (1997), then Tennessee Titans (1999) — Houston left without NFL football 5 years

Bob McNair — The Man Who Brought Football Back

Robert C. McNair paid $700M for an NFL expansion franchise in 1999
October 6, 1999: NFL owners voted unanimously for Houston team #32
September 6, 2000: Named "Houston Texans"
NRG Stadium: Opened 2002, $474M — first retractable-roof in NFL
Super Bowls: XXXVIII (2004), LI (2017) hosted at NRG
Legacy: Passed away Nov 23, 2018 — transformative figure for Houston

The McNair Family — Current Ownership

Janice McNair: Principal owner after Bob's passing
Cal McNair: CEO Jan 2019, Principal owner March 2024
Vision: New training facility in NW Harris County
Philanthropy: Janice continues Robert and Janice McNair Foundation

Key Players — Franchise Icons

David Carr: First pick (2002), #1 overall
Andre Johnson: All-time franchise leader in receptions/yards
Arian Foster: 1,616 rushing yards in 2010 (undrafted record)
J.J. Watt: 3x DPOY, most iconic Texan, Hurricane Harvey hero
DeAndre Hopkins: Elite WR, 104 rec, 1,165 yards peak seasons
DeShaun Watson: Traded to Cleveland for 3 1st-rounders + (2022)
C.J. Stroud: 2023 #2 pick, Offensive ROTY, AFC South champion
Will Anderson Jr.: 2023 Defensive ROTY, foundation piece

Franchise Milestones

2002: Beat Dallas 19-10 in first game (Sept 8)
2011: First AFC South title, first playoff win (J.J. Watt rookie)
2017: Super Bowl LI at NRG (Patriots 28-3 comeback)
2023: DeMeco Ryans era — Stroud + Will Anderson dual ROTY
2024: Second consecutive playoff appearance
2025: Beat Steelers 30-6 AFC Wild Card — first road playoff win

Mike Tomon — New President

Appointed: January 27, 2025
Georgetown: Alum, former Hoyas football
Career: Suns → Bobcats → Cavaliers → AEG → Legends → Texans
20+ years: Sports & entertainment leadership
Awards: SBJ "40 Under 40" (2015); Dallas Top 500 (2024)
Oversees: All business ops — marketing, tickets, sponsorship, community, legal, HR
Leading: NW Harris County training facility project
Expertise: Stadium development, global partnerships

Texas SB 1001 — Right to Charge

The 60-Day Rule
As of January 1, 2026, condo HOAs cannot block EV charger installation in your deeded parking spot.
Submit a written application → HOA has 60 days to respond → silence = automatic approval.
You cover installation & electricity. Must use a licensed electrician and provide proof of insurance.
Resale value: A private Level 2 charger is a major "future-proofing" asset for units in the $600K–$800K range.

NRG Green Lot — Melissa's Perk

Free Daily Charging at Work
Location: Green Lot, West VIP/Staff entry off Murworth Dr. 6 Level 2 charging ports.
Benefit: Plug-and-play for staff. A full executive workday fully recovers commute range + more. Cost: $0.
Hardware note: Ports use J1772 plugs. Keep the Tesla NACS-to-J1772 adapter in the glovebox — NRG hasn't converted all stalls yet.

Tesla Electric: The $5/Month Strategy

Plan: Tesla Electric Fixed Plan — available in CenterPoint utility territory (all of Houston).

Cost: $5/month per vehicle for unlimited overnight charging from 12:00 AM to 6:00 AM.

Condo requirement: Your parking spot charger must be tied to your unit's specific meter. When touring units at The Spires, 2520 Robinhood, or Memorial Condominiums — confirm the electrical room allows individual unit sub-metering.
